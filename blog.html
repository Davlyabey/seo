SOFTWARE IS A DOOMSDAY DEVICE
How did we get to this point?

Augusta Ada King can be considered the first person to recognize the potential of computers. She envisioned them as more than just calculating machines. Her work paved the way for what we now call software. She wrote the first algorithm for a machine to execute, known as the Analytical Engine. Born in 1815. Fast forward a century, and another woman appears on the scene: Grace Hopper. Most people know her as the creator of the COBOL language. She revolutionized computing by removing punched cards, which were used to feed data/instructions to machines. She created the first compiler for the A-0 programming language in 1952. Thus began a new era for humanity. They simply wanted to systematize the process in a work-centric way.

All computers and machines, except quantum computers, interpret only the binary language, that is, whether electricity is on or off. This is useful because we want computers to be precise, and what is more precise than verifying whether something exists or not? Compilers help computers do just that; they take code written in a higher language and convert it to a lower one (in this case, binary or machine language). It's worth noting that all languages eventually translate to binary (except quantum computers, but we'll get to that later).
